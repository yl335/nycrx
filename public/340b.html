What is 340B

Medical costs increased by 6.9% between 2009 and 2010*.
In 2010, 629,000 people in New York reported being unable to get needed medical care. In the same year, 1,028,000 people reported being uninsured.
(Source: DOH Epi Query 2010)

In response, 340B Drug Pricing Program was created by HRSA and OFA.
It offers meaningful savings to patients on their medications (average 50% off), and to generate significant revenue to the safety net providers (entities). 340B is available to all FQHCs, DSH Hospitals, FQHC look-alikes as well as several other types of providers.
Check if your organization is eligible.

However, the 340B program is currently severely underutilized.
Many safety net providers eligible for 340B aren't taking advantage of the program. Some of the reasons for not pursuing the 340B opportunity are the lack of outpatient pharmacy relationship, and perceived complexity of the program.

Savings & Costs

340B Savings
Using 340B, prescription medicine savings average about 50% off retail. A patient can expect to save the difference between retail and 340B, less fees (pharmacies collect a per prescription dispensing fee to cover their costs).

Savings = Retail cost - 340B cost - fees
Revenue = Total Collections (Copay + Commerical Reimbursement) - 340B Cost - Fees

For a retail drug that costs $100, with 340B it will cost about $50, with $10 of fees, the final savings for will be $40. As these savings become very meaningful over time. Your organization will be able to:

  • Allow more under-served patients to afford prescription medicine
  • Bring in more revenue to your organization, thus ensuring better services and care

Tailored Implementation

Every health center and hospital works differently and has different needs. With many years of experience in federal health agencies and 340B services, NYCRx work with each partner to tailor develop a program that works for you.

Pharmacy Selection
We work with your staff to identify the best local pharmacy partners for your clinic. We help either formalize an existing relationship or release an RFP to assist in the selection of a new partner.

Negotiation of Contracts and Terms
NYCRx has been helping eligible entities negotiate their contracted retail pharmacy agreements for over 5 years. We have draft contracts for entities to use at no charge, and we experience structuring agreements to fit many different configurations.

Federal Office of Pharmacy Affairs 340B Paperwork
After the contracts have been negotiated, NYCRx will help the entity stay on track with:

  • All the necessary Federal OPA paperwork
  • Wholesaler paperwork
  • Health Information Number setup
  • NYCRx reporting setup

Once all of the above accounts are established, NYCRx’s technology team will configure our systems to monitor all prescription traffic and all wholesaler transactions so our reports can reflect both dispensing data and inventory control.

Launch
Once our systems are in place to allow complete compliance, we are ready to go live. Someone from NYCRx will be available for a kick off meeting with staff and providers, and will bring marketing materials to drive traffic and ensure maximum capture.

Implementation Duration
There are quarterly submission and processing windows at the Office of Pharmacy affairs. And the process generally takes at least 90 days. For example adding a new site or adding a new pharmacy relationship to the Federal OPA’s website has an Oct 1-Oct15 deadline for recognition on Jan 1. So depending on when you begin in relation to the deadline, the process can take quite a while.
